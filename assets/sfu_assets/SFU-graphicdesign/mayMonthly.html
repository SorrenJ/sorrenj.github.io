International Graduate Students

Study permit processing times

According to the most recent processing information from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada, students outside Canada who submit a complete study permit application by May 15, 2021 will receive a final decision by August 6, 2021. Processing delays may still occur if you are unable to submit a complete application due to COVID-19 restrictions such as reduced services at visa application centres (VACs) or other providers. Applications submitted after May 15 will be processed as quickly as possible, but may or may not receive a final decision before the Fall term begins. Please visit the International Services for Students website for updates and information.

Applications for exchange now open.

Exchange for Fall 2021 and Spring 2022 is open to all eligible SFU students, including international students. For information on Study Abroad and COVID-19, please visit the website.

Programs + Resources

  • Writing Services offered by Library: The Research Commons provides writing support services to SFU graduate researchers across all disciplines.  Support includes Read-Ahead, Writing Consultations, Open Writing Lab, and Thesis Boot Camp. Find guides and resources for academic writings, including writing on thesis or dissertation or selected guides to academic writing in specific disciplines

  • The Prosody Pyramid: Tips for Speaking Effectively:  May 19 to June 23 This workshop is open to all SFU students. In this workshop series, we will review each of the four levels of the prosody pyramid-thought groups, focus words, stress, and peak—and learn to apply them to our own speech to speak with confidence and clarity.

  • Structuring Ideas Through Grammar: May 25 to June 15 This workshop is open to all SFU students. The normal organization of information in English clauses is old to new. This means that new and important information tends to appear later in the clause or sentence. Learn how to use “given/ new contract,” to organize your writing so that it conforms to the expectations of your readers.

  • ISS session recordings: The video recordings of April 8th Immigration Updates and Q&A session and April 15th Permanent Residence Workshop are now available on ISS's website. For general information and resources related to immigration policies, procedures, and application process, please connect with International student advisors at ISS.  

  • Transition Case Manager for International Graduate Students: Get one-on-one support to help you transition to campus life and managing daily challenges. Book an an appointment: 778-782-4615
  • International Teaching Assistants Program: Free 10-week seminar to help TAs and graduate students who speak English as an additional language with their academic communication and teaching skills. Learn more and register today!
  • LiveChat with an International Student Advisor: Discuss your immigration-related questions, medical insurance and healthcare questions, and gain referrals for intercultural transition support.
